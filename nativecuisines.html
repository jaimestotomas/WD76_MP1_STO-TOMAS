NATIVE CUISINES

ICE CREAM VOLCANO

It is one of Bicol's original desserts. Ice cream with a kick of Sili. Invented by the 1st Colonial Grill in 2004, the sili ice cream, has used the local symbol of Bicol, the chilli, and created a unique ice cream flavour.

DILIS

Dilis are a Filipino delicacy consisting of anchovies, often dried, that are lightly battered before being deep-fried and served hot as an appetizer.


LAING

Why is laing famous in Bicol? Laing (or Natong) is a spicy dish that is Coconut milk (or cream) based with dried taro leaves. This originated from the Philippine's Bicol region.

BICOL EXPRESS

Bicol Express, known natively in Bikol as sinilihan, is a popular Filipino dish which was popularized in the district of Malate, Manila but made in traditional Bicolano style.

DAING

Daing, tuyô, or bilad are dried fish from the Philippines. Fish prepared as daing are usually split open, gutted, salted liberally, and then sun and air-dried.

SINAPOT

The Sinapot are usually made from saba bananas. In the Bicol version, it does not “fan” the bananas. Instead they are simply sliced lengthwise before frying in batter.
